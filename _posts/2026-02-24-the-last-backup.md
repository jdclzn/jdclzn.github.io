**Drafts & Dragons** — *Field Notes from a Developer Learning About Irreversible Things*

There’s a rule in software everyone learns eventually.

Backups are useless if you never test them.

You can have the most perfect backup system in the world — automated, redundant, replicated across regions — but if the day comes and you can’t restore from it, then it was all just an illusion.

A false sense of safety.

A lie you told yourself so you could sleep at night.

I didn’t realize until now that memories are the only backups we get.

And they’re already degrading.

---

## I Keep Searching for an Earlier Version of Her

These days, I keep looking at my mother like she’s a system that’s slowly losing uptime.

She’s still here.

Still responding.

Still smiling.

But there’s latency now.

There’s fragility.

There are moments where she goes quiet, and I wonder if something inside her just… stopped.

Sometimes she looks smaller.

Not physically.

Existentially.

Like parts of her are already being decommissioned.

And I hate myself for noticing.

Because noticing makes it real.

---

## The Lie of “Later”

I used to believe in later.

Later, I’ll take her somewhere nice.

Later, I’ll spend more time at home.

Later, I’ll repay everything she sacrificed.

Later was my infinite storage.

Unlimited capacity.

No expiration.

Now I know later was never guaranteed.

Later was just a variable that was never initialized.

---

## The Sound of Her Voice Is Already Becoming a Backup

I’ve started paying attention to things I never noticed before.

The exact way she says my name.

The way her voice softens when she’s worried about me.

The way she asks if I’ve eaten.

The way she still treats me like I’m a child, even though I’m old enough to understand what’s happening.

I try to memorize it.

Every detail.

Every inflection.

Because I know someday, this will be all I have left.

Audio files in my head.

Corrupted over time.

Losing clarity.

Becoming incomplete.

---

## I Hate That I’m Thinking Like This While She’s Still Here

She’s still alive.

Still breathing.

Still here.

And already, part of me is backing her up.

Archiving her.

Preparing for a failure that hasn’t happened yet.

I hate it.

It feels like betrayal.

Like I’m giving up.

Like I’m accepting a future I don’t want.

But I can’t stop.

Because I’ve spent my whole life in disaster recovery.

And I know what happens when you don’t prepare.

You lose everything.

---

## Three Years Without a Backup

The hardest part isn’t just that she’s sick.

It’s that she was sick alone.

For three years.

Three years without telling anyone.

Three years of silent failure.

Three years without redundancy.

Without replication.

Without help.

She carried it all herself.

Because she was protecting us.

Because she thought losing her too would destroy us.

She was right.

It is destroying me.

Just slower.

---

## I Keep Replaying Old Snapshots

I remember her waiting for me to come home from school.

I remember her cooking my favorite food.

I remember her staying awake when I was sick.

I remember her being strong when my brother died.

Even when she was breaking.

She never showed it.

She was always the most reliable system in my life.

99.999% uptime.

Always available.

Always there.

Until now.

---

## There Is No Restore Button

I’ve spent weeks wishing for impossible things.

If I could just go back.

If I could just find it sooner.

If I could just force a restore.

If I could just rewrite the timeline.

But time isn’t version-controlled.

There is no Git for reality.

There is no:

`git checkout 2022`

There is no branch where my brother is still alive.

There is no branch where my mother isn’t sick.

There is only main.

And main is unforgiving.

---

## She Still Asks If I’m Okay

Even now.

Even like this.

She asks me if I’m okay.

She asks if I’m tired.

She asks if I’m working too hard.

She’s the one dying.

And she’s worried about me.

I don’t understand how someone can love like that.

I don’t understand how someone can be that selfless.

I don’t understand how the world can take someone like her.

It doesn’t make sense.

There’s no logic.

No fairness.

Just loss.

---

## I’m Afraid of the Moment the System Goes Silent

I’m afraid of the last conversation.

Because I won’t know it’s the last.

I’m afraid of the last time she says my name.

The last time she smiles.

The last time she exists.

I’m afraid of the moment she becomes a memory.

A backup.

Static data.

Read-only.

Untouchable.

Gone.

---

## I’m Trying to Save Everything

So now I stay.

I sit beside her.

Even in silence.

Especially in silence.

Because silence is still data.

Still presence.

Still something.

I listen to her stories.

Even the ones I’ve heard a hundred times.

I let her repeat them.

Because someday, I’ll wish I could hear them again.

I memorize her existence.

I create as many backups as I can.

Even though I know none of them will ever be enough.

---

## The Truth About Backups

Backups don’t prevent loss.

They only preserve what’s already gone.

They’re not a solution.

They’re a consolation.

A way to remember what you couldn’t save.

---

## If There Is a God, I Hope He Keeps Better Backups Than I Do

Because I’m not ready.

I will never be ready.

There is no amount of preparation that makes this easier.

No documentation.

No runbook.

No guide.

Just love.

And the slow, inevitable realization that love cannot stop time.

---

## For Now, She Is Still Here

For now, the system is still running.

For now, her voice still exists outside my memory.

For now, I don’t need the backup.

For now, I still have the original.

So I stay.

And I sit beside her.

And I try to be brave.

Even though I know, someday, all I’ll have left…

is the last backup.
